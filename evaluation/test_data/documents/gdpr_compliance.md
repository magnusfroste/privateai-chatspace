# GDPR Compliance Guide

## Overview

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that came into effect on May 25, 2018. It applies to all organizations processing personal data of EU residents.

---

## Key Articles

### Article 5 - Principles of Processing

Personal data must be:
1. Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently
2. Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
3. Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary
4. Accurate and kept up to date
5. Kept for no longer than necessary
6. Processed securely

### Article 6 - Lawful Basis for Processing

Processing is lawful only if one of the following applies:
- Consent from the data subject
- Necessary for contract performance
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests
- Public interest
- Legitimate interests

### Article 7 - Conditions for Consent

Consent requirements:
- Must be freely given
- Must be specific and informed
- Must be unambiguous
- Can be withdrawn at any time
- Burden of proof lies with the controller

### Article 12 - Transparent Communication

Controllers must provide information in:
- A concise, transparent, intelligible manner
- Clear and plain language
- Writing or electronic means
- Free of charge (with exceptions)

### Article 15 - Right of Access

Data subjects have the right to:
- Confirm whether their data is being processed
- Access their personal data
- Receive information about processing purposes
- Know the categories of data concerned
- Know recipients of the data

### Article 17 - Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

Data subjects can request deletion when:
- Data is no longer necessary for original purpose
- Consent is withdrawn
- Data subject objects to processing
- Data was unlawfully processed
- Legal obligation requires erasure

**Response time:** Controllers must respond within **30 days**.

**Exceptions:**
- Freedom of expression
- Legal obligations
- Public health
- Archiving purposes
- Legal claims

### Article 20 - Right to Data Portability

Data subjects can:
- Receive their data in a structured, machine-readable format
- Transmit data to another controller
- Request direct transmission between controllers

### Article 25 - Data Protection by Design

Controllers must implement:
- Appropriate technical measures
- Appropriate organizational measures
- Data minimization principles
- Privacy by default

### Article 32 - Security of Processing

Security measures must include:
- Pseudonymization and encryption
- Confidentiality, integrity, availability
- Ability to restore data
- Regular testing and evaluation

### Article 33 - Breach Notification

In case of a personal data breach:
- Notify supervisory authority within **72 hours**
- Document all breaches
- Notify data subjects if high risk

### Article 35 - Data Protection Impact Assessment

DPIA required when processing:
- Involves systematic evaluation of personal aspects
- Involves large-scale processing of special categories
- Involves systematic monitoring of public areas

## Data Retention Guidelines

### Recommended Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| Customer records | 7 years after last transaction | Tax/accounting laws |
| Employee records | 6 years after employment ends | Employment law |
| Marketing consent | Until withdrawn | GDPR Article 7 |
| Website logs | 90 days | Legitimate interest |
| CCTV footage | 30 days | Legitimate interest |
| Contract documents | 10 years after expiry | Limitation periods |

### Deletion Procedures

1. Identify data for deletion
2. Verify no legal holds apply
3. Execute secure deletion
4. Document the deletion
5. Notify relevant parties

## Fines and Penalties

### Tier 1 Violations (Lower)
- Up to €10 million or 2% of annual turnover
- Examples: Inadequate records, failure to notify breach

### Tier 2 Violations (Higher)
- Up to €20 million or 4% of annual turnover
- Examples: Unlawful processing, violating data subject rights

- [ ] Appoint Data Protection Officer (if required)
- [ ] Maintain Records of Processing Activities

- [ ] Provide privacy notices
- [ ] Establish data subject rights procedures
- [ ] Implement security measures
- [ ] Conduct DPIAs where required
- [ ] Establish breach notification procedures
- [ ] Review data retention policies
- [ ] Train staff on GDPR requirements
